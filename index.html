Who am I?
Hi, I'm Joyce Fan. A Chinese Canadian on a mission to explore and taste the world's best eats! I love discovering hidden gems and sharing honest reviews with fellow food lovers.
I'm a Global Business and Digital Arts student at the University of Waterloo, and I'm passionate about exploring the world through food. Just like I blend art, design, and storytelling in my creative projects, I love sharing the stories behind the dishes I try — the flavours, the people, and the culture that make every meal special.
Where I am from
I was born and raised in Canada, though I traveled a lot with my family when I was younger — most of those memories are a blur, but I do remember enjoying all the food along the way! Growing up, I was always drawn to creativity, whether through drawing, music, or dance, and that curiosity has naturally extended to the food I eat. Being the youngest of three, I've always had the guidance and care of my older siblings, and their influence has shaped not only who I am as a person, but also how I explore the world — with curiosity, openness, and a taste for adventure.
Where I'm going
When it comes to food, my tastes have evolved over time. As a kid, I loved sweets and fast food and was a picky eater, only enjoying certain meats, vegetables, and seafood. But as I grew older, I started experimenting, discovering flavours and dishes I never thought I'd like. Now, I love to eat around, visit new places, and experience the flavours people grow up with. East Asian cuisine is my personal favourite — the balance of flavours, textures, and colours never fails to excite me.
Through this blog, I want to share my food adventures, my honest reviews, and the little stories behind every dish I try. My goal is to discover new flavours, explore hidden gems, and connect with others who share my love of food. Whether it's a cozy café, a bustling street market, or a fine dining experience, I hope my journey inspires you to taste boldly, explore widely, and enjoy every bite along the way.
How does the rating work?
I review each dish using four key categories:

  1. Presentation
  • How the dish looks - plating, colour, and visual appeal.
  1. Taste
  • Flavour, texture, and overall enjoyment.
  1. Value
  • How worth it the price is for the quality and portion.
    1 = not expensive / good value
    10 = very expensive
  1. Recommendation
  • Would I suggest this to someone?
  • Should Try - I recommend it
  • Could Try - optional, depends on your taste
  • Must Try - I am telling you to go and try it out

Overall Rating
A combined score that reflects my full experience with the dish.
